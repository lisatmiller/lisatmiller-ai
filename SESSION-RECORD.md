## PART ONE. SETTLED DECISIONS AND FINAL LANGUAGE

These are the locked outcomes of the session. Everything in this section was explicitly decided by Lisa.

**The positioning line (FINAL, exact wording):**
> The practical application of AI for the business of healthcare

Word choices tested and resolved: "application" beats "use" (discipline versus activity). "For" beats "on." "Business of healthcare" chosen over "business side of healthcare" for the final line (broader, established executive phrase). Short form for bios and casual use: "Practical AI for the business of healthcare."

**The firm identity:** The venture runs under Lisa's personal and professional brand, Lisa T. Miller. No new firm name. The web property is lisatmiller.ai (mini site, separate from lisatmiller.com which keeps its sales-strategy positioning). Coined name "Workflowe" is parked in reserve for a possible future product, tool, or spin-out.

**The niche:** Business-side (non-clinical) workflows in healthcare. Revenue cycle, patient access, prior authorization, supply chain, finance and accounting, workforce operations. Explicitly NOT clinical AI.

**Language currently ON HOLD (do not use publicly yet, per Lisa):** "Workflow Economics" (category name), "Workflow Debt" / "Manual Workflow Debt" (condition name), "Workflow Economics Audit" (product name). The word "workflow" may be used descriptively but not as branded language.

**Approved key language (Lisa-approved, usable):**
- "AI can now do real work inside a health system. Most of it hasn't started yet." (homepage subline, approved verbatim)
- "Humans absorb bad workflows." (evolved from "hide," which Lisa rejected as negative; "absorb" approved as blameless)
- "Most haven't seen what AI can absorb now." (second half of couplet)
- The human subsidy concept: "Every broken process in an enterprise is currently subsidized by human adaptability, people who work around it, catch its errors, absorb its friction, without ever billing that effort to anyone."
- "Your best people are absorbing your worst workflows" / site-safe version "Your best people quietly carry your most broken processes."
- Restoration frame: AI returns capacity, hours, attention to the organization and its people.
- "The work AI does best is the work nobody went into healthcare to do."

**Style rules in force for all content:** No em dashes. No colons in headings or body copy. Never "bounce back" for readmissions. "We" voice for the firm (Lisa and her team). Word .docx for long-form deliverables.

**The offer structure (as built into the site):**
1. The Opportunity Assessment. Rapid assessment of business operations, maps where manual work concentrates, what it costs annually, which processes AI can carry today. Deliverable is a prioritized opportunity analysis priced in dollars and hours, client keeps findings either way.
2. Targeted implementation. Deploy against top opportunities alongside client teams, measured against established baselines, sequenced to prove value early.
3. Team enablement. Train client staff to run, supervise, and extend what was built.

**Stats used on the site (flagged for verification before launch):** ~25% of U.S. hospital spending on administration (Health Affairs), ~$20B/yr hospital spend pursuing denial appeals (Premier), ~13 hrs/week physician and staff time on prior authorization (AMA survey), $25+ average rework cost per denied claim (HFMA benchmarks).
